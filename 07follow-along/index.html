Our Story

Founded in 1782, Angelo got his start selling biscuits to revolution.

As a mark of defiance against the filthy loyalist Torys, Angelo heroically decided to sart crafting his biscuits to be flaky and buttery, rather than full of chocolate.

Due to his efforts to offend the British, George Washington himself awarded Angelo with the fledgling nation's thirty-fourth medal of honor for services rendered. This momentous occassion marked the first time in history a black man was recongnized for doing anything, and effectively launched the end of racism.
